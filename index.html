Transport in Australia

Overview

This dashboard explores Australia's transport landscape through interactive visuals, showing how people move and what influences their choices. It compares transport use by state, age, employment, and income, alongside trends in vehicle ownership and accident rates. The visuals paint a picture of how geography, lifestyle, and social factors shape movement across the country.

Transport Type

State Transport Usage

This map shows the proportion of each transport type within total transport use by state. Western Australia has the highest share of train travel relative to other states, while nearly 90% of transport in Tasmania is by car. The Northern Territory stands out with almost double the walking rate of any other state.

Income Distribution

This heatmap shows the most common income levels among people who primarily use different types of transport to get to work. Truck drivers earn fairly consistently within a narrow income range, while cyclists tend to be higher earners. Bus users are spread across all income levels, showing that public transport appeals to a wide range of earners. Car users generally cluster in the middle-to-upper income brackets.

Transport by Age

This line graph shows the most common modes of transport, as a percentage of each age group's total transport use, excluding cars (which dominate overall). Younger people tend to favour walking, trains, and buses, though the latter two drop off sharply with age. Older groups rely more on walking, with truck use rising steadily. Bus use declines consistently across age groups, while bicycle use fluctuates, peaking around middle age.

Ownership & Accidents

Vehicle Ownership

This stacked bar chart shows how many households own different numbers of vehicles, broken down by household composition. Households with one or two vehicles dominate, accounting for over 6.7 million combined. Single occupants and multi-family homes show lower ownership rates, while lone-person households represent the largest share of zero-vehicle households. You can click a household composition in the legend to explore the data further.

Transport by State

This bubble chart shows the relationship between the number of vehicles per 1,000 people and road deaths per 10,000 people in each state. The size of each bubble represents the total kilometres travelled within that state. NSW and QLD lead in vehicle usage with high kilometers traveled, while SA shows elevated road fatality rates despite similar vehicle ownership. ACT has the lowest fatality rate.

Vehicles Per Capita

This graph shows the number of each vehicle type per capita across states. The overall ordering is similar for all states, though the Northern Territory stands out with noticeably fewer vehicles per person, likely due to its low population density. Light commercial vehicles vary the most, with Tasmania recording the highest rate.

Accidents by Vehicle

This stacked bar chart shows the number of road accidents by vehicle group and type. Total accidents increased from about 22,000 in 2011 to 26,000 in 2021. Light vehicles, such as bicycles, motorbikes, and pedestrians, show steady growth and remain the most at risk. Heavy and other vehicle accidents stayed relatively stable over the same period.

Employment & Economy

Employment Over Time

This area chart shows the share of total industry employment held by each specific sub-industry. Road transport dominates at approximately 70% of employment throughout 2000-2024. Rail employment shows minor fluctuations around 8-10%, while other and water transport remain negligible components of total transport employment.

Earnings by Mode

Other transport accounts for the largest earnings shares, with an unstable proportion, whereas the others maintain relatively stable proportions. Air transport earnings proportion grew from 2008-2014 before stabilizing, reflecting post-GFC recovery patterns.

Gender in Employment

This horizontal bar chart shows the proportion of employment by gender within specific transport sub-industries and across the transport industry as a whole. While gender balance varies widely between sub-industries, the overall transport sector remains consistently male-dominated, at roughly 80% male and 20% female. See "Total" in 2021 for comments.

Freight & GDP

This combined line and bar chart shows how different transport types within the industry contribute to GDP. Most transport modes track closely with GDP growth, rising sharply before the GFC. GDP itself fell in 2020 yet local delivery (Road) actually saw an increase.
